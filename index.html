I am Yibo Peng, a Research Assistant at Carnegie Mellon University. Advised by Prof. Beidi Chen, my research focuses on the security of AI agents. I also collaborate with Prof. Huaxiu Yao on multimodel large language models and with Prof. Graham Neubig on code agents through the OpenHands. Previously, I was a research intern at Microsoft Research.

I recently received my Master's degree in Artificial Intelligence Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University. During my studies, I had the privilege of being advised by Prof. Daniel Fried, with a focus on Code Generation and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).

My research interests lie in addressing the core challenges for AI agents: enhancing their multimodal reasoning capabilities in complex environments, optimizing the efficiency of multi-agent collaboration, and ensuring the safety of their behavior.

I am actively seeking Ph.D. opportunities for the Fall 2026 admission cycle. If you are interested in my work or potential collaborations, please feel free to contact me via email.
