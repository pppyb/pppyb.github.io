Yibo Peng (彭艺博)

彭艺博 · Péng Yìbó (pronounced "penɡ EE-bor")

I am Yibo Peng, a Research Assistant at Carnegie Mellon University. Advised by Prof. Beidi Chen, my research focuses on the security of AI agents(https://github.com/Infini-AI-Lab/FCV). I also collaborate with Prof. Huaxiu Yao on multimodel large language models (https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.00555) and with Prof. Graham Neubig(https://www.phontron.com/) on code agents through the OpenHands(https://docs.all-hands.dev/). Previously, I was a research intern at Microsoft Research.

I recently received my Master’s degree in Artificial Intelligence Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University. During my studies, I had the privilege of being advised by Prof. Daniel Fried(link: https://dpfried.github.io/), with a focus on Code Generation and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)(link: https://openreview.net/pdf?id=pmcWo9DtDw).

My research interests lie in addressing the core challenges for AI agents: enhancing their multimodal reasoning capabilities in complex environments, optimizing the efficiency of multi-agent collaboration, and ensuring the safety of their behavior.

I am actively seeking Ph.D. opportunities for the Fall 2026 admission cycle. If you are interested in my work or potential collaborations, please feel free to contact me via email(link：yibopcmu@gmail.com).
