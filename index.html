About

I am Yibo Peng, a Research Assistant at Carnegie Mellon University. Advised by Prof. Beidi Chen, my research focuses on the AI security in real-world applications. I am also expanding my research through upcoming collaborations with Prof. David Wagner (UC Berkeley) and Prof. Bo Li (UIUC), and I actively collaborate with Prof. Huaxiu Yao on multimodal large language models.

I obtained my Master's degree in Artificial Intelligence Engineering from CMU. During my graduate studies, I worked under the supervision of Prof. Daniel Fried, focusing on code generation and RAG. Additionally, I contributed to the OpenHands project (focusing on code agents) under the mentorship of Prof. Graham Neubig.

Broadly, my research aims to address core challenges for AI agents: enhancing multimodal reasoning, optimizing collaboration, and ensuring safety and reliability.
